Why Bubl can help you slay Dragons...

Terra Incognita

Since early recorded history humankind has understood both the intrigue but also the inherent fears rooted in unknown territory. First seen in Ptolemy's Geography circa 150 and later introduced again in the 15th century, the term "Terra Incognita" can be translated as "unknown land". Originally used in early cartography for regions not yet mapped, urban legend claims such regions were also labelled with the phrase "Here be dragons". Only one surviving map substantiates that legend, but ancient Roman and Medieval cartographers were well known to use the phrase "HIC SVNT LEONES" on their own maps - "Here are Lions".

Our development team is made up entirely of ex-junior high students who transitioned from junior high into our own "Terra Incognita". Highschool lands mostly uncharted territory. We have heard the stories about Dragons and Lions, and navigated them. Like the early explorers who steeled themselves into unknown lands we too have traveled the halls of our schools, mapping them in our minds by our own experiences.

Bubls mission is to create some friendly landmarks while you step into your own "Terra Incognita" and begin mapping your new terrain.
